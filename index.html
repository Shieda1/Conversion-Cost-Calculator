What is the conversion cost?
Conversion costs are the production costs that must be available in order to convert the raw materials into completed products.
How to calculate conversion cost?
To calculate conversion cost you should use the following formula:
conversion cost = (direct labor overhead + manufacturing) / units produced
Example: Comapny x has $80,000 in direct labor, it also has $110,000 in factory overhead costs and the company has produced 32,000 units in the current month, calculate the conversion cost?
Using the conversion cost formula the answer will be:
conversion cost = (direct labor overhead + manufacturing) / units produced
conversion cost = ($80,000 + $110,000) / 32,000
conversion cost = $5.93 per unit
Why use conversion cost?
Companies use conversion costs in order to determine the efficiency in the production process and the managers use it to find out where might be a waste in the manufacturing process.
